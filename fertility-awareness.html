What is Fertility Awareness?

Fertility Awareness

Fertility awareness involves daily observation of your body's signs of fertility to determine whether or not you are fertile that specific day. The most common signs of fertility that are tracked are cervical mucus, basal body temperature, and cervical position.

Cervical Mucus

The most important sign of fertility, cervical mucus signals the start of your fertile window. In response to rising estrogen levels before ovulation both the color and consistency of cervical mucus changes from cloudy with little stretch to clear, lubricative and very stretchy.

Basal Body Temperature

A helpful tool for confirming when ovulation has happened, observing basal body temperature measurements reveals the shift from the typically lower temperatures of the follicular (pre-ovulatory) phase to the higher temperatures of the luteal (post-ovulatory) phase.

Cervical Position

Often seen as an optional sign of fertility to track, observing cervical position can be helpful additional information. Rising estrogen levels leading up to ovulation cause the cervix to soften and slightly open. After ovulation the rise in progesterone levels cause the cervix to feel firm and to close.

The Justisse Method

A sympto-thermal fertility awareness method, the Justisse method is unique in its emphasis on holistic sexual and reproductive health. Working with an HRHP (Holistic Reproductive Health Practitioner) can help you to not only learn the to chart your cycles but to also understand how to interpret your menstrual cycle charts.

As mentioned in the Justisse User Guide, “Justisse HRHPs are trained to be receptive to a woman’s particular cultural, spiritual, or philosophical beliefs.” This means you can count on your HRHP to support you in your reproductive health goals regardless of your relationship status, political beliefs, gender, or any other area of diversity.
